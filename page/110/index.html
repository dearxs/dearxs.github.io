关于年轻还是衰老

发表于 2018-06-24 更新于 2020-03-17 分类于 观点 Disqus：

那天出门骑着共享单车，好容易才出一次门，去吃路边摊的烧烤和炒河粉。想着自己的状态，忽然就想起了大学一年级写作老师硬是让我们背的那一首小诗“Youth”。当时那位年轻的老师说，多年以后你们可能大学里所有的事情都忘了，但是你们肯定能想起来这一首小诗。

她说得对。

那位年轻老师的面容我已经基本忘记了，只记得她目光炯炯，上课总是神采奕奕，精神亢奋。她的履历应该也是很风光的，至少在我们那个二三流的大学里面是这样。

那首小诗的第一段我还大概可以背的出，但是后面的已经忘得差不多了。不过，第一句总是可以脱口而出的。

Youth is not a time of life; it is a state of mind.

吃完路边摊会去就顺手把那首小诗搜出来诵读了几遍。对比一下自己的生活状态，羞愧万分。自己虽然年纪轻轻，但是其实心里已经老死了。贴出来这首小诗，警示自己。

YOUTH by Samuel Ullman

Youth is not a time of life; it is a state of mind; it is not a matter of rosy cheeks, red lips and supple knees; it is a matter of the will, a quality of the imagination, a vigor of the emotions; it is the freshness of the deep springs of life.

Youth means a temperamental predominance of courage over timidity of the appetite, for adventure over the love of ease. This often exists in a man of sixty more than a boy of twenty. Nobody grows old merely by a number of years. We grow old by deserting our ideals.

Years may wrinkle the skin, but to give up enthusiasm wrinkles the soul. Worry, fear, self-distrust bows the heart and turns the spirit back to dust.

Whether sixty or sixteen, there is in every human being’s heart the lure of wonder, the unfailing child-like appetite of what’s next, and the joy of the game of living. In the center of your heart and my heart there is a wireless station; so long as it receives messages of beauty, hope, cheer, courage and power from men and from the infinite, so long are you young.

When the aerials are down, and your spirit is covered with snows of cynicism and the ice of pessimism, then you are grown old, even at twenty, but as long as your aerials are up, to catch the waves of optimism, there is hope you may die young at eighty.

感谢那位老师，也希望自己可以永远年轻。
