宗教和意识形态有什么区别？

Posted on 2019-08-19 In 日记

最近看历史比较多，玩文明也会有宗教胜利，但轶事形态是归到政策下面的。所以比较纳闷宗教和意识形态的区别到底在哪里。在我看来，宗教和意识形态很像，十分相似，就像马克思主义，以及佛教，只不过信的东西不一样而已。

于是搜索了一下，Quora 上面有个回答提到 ，”ideology is a religion if and only if it is theocratic“。还提到了写《人类简史》的尤瓦尔关于这个的观点， 基本观点也是一致，很相似，只不过宗教相信一个超人法则（superhuman laws）或者说是神谕（divine laws），而意识形态相信的是一个自然法则(natural laws)。无论是哪种，都是为了规范人类的处事方式和价值观，稳固人类的机构治理。

原文如下，

Religion is any system of human norms and values that is founded on a belief in superhuman laws. Religion tells us that we must obey certain laws that were not invented by humans, and that humans cannot change at will. Some religions, such as Islam, Christianity and Hinduism, believe that these super-human laws were created by the gods. Other religions, such as Buddhism, Communism and Nazism, believe that these super-human laws are natural laws. Thus Buddhists believe in the natural laws of karma, Nazis argued that their ideology reflected the laws of natural selection, and Communists believe that they follow the natural laws of economics.

No matter whether they believe in divine laws or in natural laws, all religions have exactly the same function: to give legitimacy to human norms and values, and to give stability to human institutions such as states and corporations. Without some kind of religion, it is simply impossible to maintain social order. During the modern era religions that believe in divine laws went into eclipse. But religions that believe in natural laws became ever more powerful. In the future, they are likely to become more powerful yet. Silicon Valley, for example, is today a hot-house of new techno-religions, that promise humankind paradise here on earth with the help of new technology.
